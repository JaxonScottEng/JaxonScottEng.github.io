Robot Arm + Inverse Kinematics
6‑DOF desktop manipulator with custom IK, calibration, and Arduino control

Overview

I designed and built a compact 6‑DOF robotic arm and implemented inverse kinematics to position the end‑effector precisely in 3D space. The system blends mechanical design, control electronics, and math: forward/pose estimation, inverse kinematics, and synchronized motion profiles. The goal was smooth, accurate positioning with approachable hardware and code.

  • Degrees of Freedom: 6 (stepper base + 5 servo joints)
  • Controller: Arduino Uno (serial‑driven)
  • Drivers: TMC2209 (NEMA 17 base), hobby servos for remaining joints
  • Sensors: Calibrated servo angles; optional IMU glove input
  • Materials: 3D‑printed PLA/PETG, aluminum hardware
  • Software: Python (IK + GUI), Arduino C++ (motion)
